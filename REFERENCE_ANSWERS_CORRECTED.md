# HOTEL READY - REFERENCE ANSWERS (CORRECTED)
## All 36 Scenarios - Complete Rebuild with Proper Operational Model

**Version:** 2.0 | **Date:** June 25, 2026  
**Model:** Professional hospitality standards with reception as single contact point

---

## CRITICAL OPERATIONAL PRINCIPLES

### 1. Reception is the ONLY Guest Contact Point
- Guests communicate exclusively with **Reception** (or staff they encounter)
- Reception handles guest communication throughout
- Reception coordinates internally with other departments
- Guest never contacts other departments directly

### 2. Complaint Classification & Escalation

**MILD Complaints** (Front-line staff handle)
- Front-line staff can resolve directly
- No policy exceptions needed
- Guest remains calm/cooperative
- Solution found quickly

**SEVERE Complaints** (Manager on Duty escalates)
- Billing disputes
- Guest wants to checkout/leave
- Health/safety issues
- Overbooking or major service failures
- Demanding compensation or policy exceptions
- Threatening bad reviews or complaints

### 3. Manager on Duty Responsibilities
- Authority to make decisions
- Can offer compensation/exceptions
- Trained in complaint resolution
- Takes direct control of guest communication for severe issues
- Can coordinate cross-departmental solutions

## FD-01: Room Not Ready at Check-in

**SEVERITY:** MILD  
**HANDLER:** Receptionist (can resolve without manager)

**SITUATION:**  
Guest arrives at 2pm after 14-hour flight. Room isn't ready; will be 40 minutes.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Receptionist acknowledges frustration, apologises, offers concrete options, coordinates with housekeeping, keeps guest comfortable.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RECEPTIONIST RESPONSE:**

> "I completely understand your frustration after such a long journey. I sincerely apologize for the delay. Here's what I can do right now: I'll store your bags securely, and you're welcome to freshen up in our guest lounge with complimentary refreshments and Wi-Fi. I'll coordinate with our housekeeping team and personally call you the moment your room is ready—typically within 40 minutes. We'll make sure this doesn't inconvenience your stay further."

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Empathy and acknowledgment
- ✓ Sincere apology
- ✓ Multiple concrete comfort options
- ✓ Shows housekeeping coordination (internal)
- ✓ Personal follow-up commitment
- ✓ Clear timeline
- ✓ Warm, hospitality-focused tone

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RECEPTIONIST RESPONSE:**

> "I'm sorry your room isn't ready yet. You've traveled a long time and I understand your frustration. Your room should be ready in about 40 minutes. Would you like to wait in the lounge, or I can store your bags and you can explore the area?"

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ No mention of internal coordination
- ✗ Less comprehensive comfort options
- ✗ Weaker follow-up commitment

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RECEPTIONIST RESPONSE:**

> "Your room will be ready soon. It should be around 2:45. You can wait in the lobby or come back later."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ No empathy or acknowledgment
- ✗ No apology
- ✗ No comfort options offered
- ✗ Scripted, unhelpful tone
