# PART 1: FRONT DESK (9 Scenarios)

## FD-01: Room not ready at check-in (frustrated guest)

**SITUATION:**  
Guest arrives at 2pm after 14-hour flight. Room isn't ready and won't be for 40 minutes.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Acknowledges feelings first, apologises without over-grovelling, offers concrete next step and small goodwill gesture, sets clear expectation, stays warm and in control.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "I completely understand your frustration after such a long journey. I sincerely apologize for the delay. Here's what I can do right now: I'll store your bags securely, and you're welcome to freshen up in our guest lounge with complimentary refreshments and Wi-Fi. I'll personally text you the moment your room is ready—typically within 40 minutes. I'll also note this for our manager to ensure you're taken care of."

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Empathy and acknowledgment of feelings
- ✓ Sincere apology (without over-grovelling)
- ✓ Multiple concrete options
- ✓ Personal ownership/commitment
- ✓ Clear timeline with specific estimate
- ✓ Goodwill gesture (manager note/attention)

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "I'm sorry your room isn't ready yet. You've traveled a long time and I get why you're frustrated. Your room should be ready in about 40 minutes. Would you like to wait in the lounge, or I can store your bags and you can explore the area?"

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Personal ownership/manager note for follow-up
- ✗ Warmth and genuine hospitality tone
- ✗ Specific goodwill gesture or incentive

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Your room will be ready soon. It should be around 2:45. You can wait in the lobby or come back later."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ No acknowledgement of guest's feelings
- ✗ No apology
- ✗ No goodwill gesture or empathy
- ✗ Scripted, cold, transactional tone

## FD-02: Upsell a suite/upgrade at check-in

**SITUATION:**  
Couple arrives for weekend stay. Booked in standard room. Junior suite available at modest uplift.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Congratulates couple, frames upgrade as special anniversary experience, states price clearly without pressure, accepts 'no' gracefully, makes them feel special.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "How wonderful—congratulations on your anniversary! You know, we have a beautiful junior suite available that overlooks the garden. It's just $60 more per night, and honestly, it's the perfect setting for a special evening. But your standard room is lovely too, so whatever feels right for you. Either way, we're thrilled to have you celebrate with us."

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Warm, genuine congratulations
- ✓ Experience framing (not pure sales pitch)
- ✓ Personalized to the occasion
- ✓ Clear pricing ($60/night)
- ✓ Low-pressure language with graceful out
- ✓ Inclusive ending (special either way)

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Congratulations! We actually have an upgrade available—a junior suite for an extra $60 a night if you're interested. Would that interest you?"

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Experience framing (sounds like sales pitch)
- ✗ Personal recommendation/enthusiasm
- ✗ Graceful acceptance of 'no'

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "We have a suite upgrade available. It's $60 more. Would you like it?"

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Transactional tone
- ✗ No personalization to occasion
- ✗ No warmth or hospitality
- ✗ Pure sales pitch without emotion

## FD-03: Offer late checkout or add-ons

**SITUATION:**  
Guest calls evening before checkout. Flight at 6pm. Staff has chance to offer late checkout and add-on services.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Spots opportunity, offers late checkout warmly, suggests relevant add-on, states costs clearly, makes guest feel looked after not upsold.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Perfect timing—with a 6pm flight, you have plenty of time. How about we arrange a 2pm late checkout for you? That gives you the whole morning and early afternoon to relax. We also have a wonderful spa package—a 50-minute massage for $85—or you could grab lunch in our restaurant from 11am. Either way, no pressure—just want to make sure you're not rushing."

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Proactive opportunity-spotting
- ✓ Warm, helpful tone
- ✓ Specific options with clear pricing
- ✓ Multiple value-add choices
- ✓ "No pressure" language throughout
- ✓ Value-focused, not fee-focused

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Since your flight is at 6pm, I can arrange a 2pm late checkout if you'd like. We have a spa and restaurant as well if you want to relax before you go."

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Enthusiasm about the opportunity
- ✗ Specific pricing and options
- ✗ "No pressure" reassurance

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "You can check out at 2pm if you want. That will be an extra charge."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Focuses on fee/cost, not value
- ✗ Not service-minded tone
- ✗ No helpful or warm approach
- ✗ Transactional language

## FD-04: Billing dispute at checkout

**SITUATION:**  
Guest questions minibar charge and spa cancellation fee. Not angry but clearly concerned.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Listens without defensiveness, reviews each charge methodically, acknowledges concern, offers to investigate, resolves/escalates with clear next steps.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Thank you for bringing these to my attention. Let me review these with you right now. The minibar charge—I see it here on your bill. Let me confirm exactly what you're looking at. And the spa cancellation—that's a great question. I can see it was charged, but I want to make sure that's correct. If you weren't notified about a cancellation fee, I'd like to investigate that with our spa manager. Can we resolve the minibar together right now, and then I'll look into the spa charge immediately and follow up with you today?"

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Calm reception without defensiveness
- ✓ Methodical charge-by-charge review
- ✓ Specific acknowledgement of each concern
- ✓ Willingness to investigate both charges
- ✓ Clear follow-up timeline (today)
- ✓ Ownership mindset

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Of course, let me look at those charges. I see the minibar here. And the spa cancellation—let me check into that for you. One is definitely on your bill, but I can investigate the other one."

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Full methodical review structure
- ✗ Clear follow-up commitment/timeline
- ✗ Specific investigation promise

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "That's what the system shows. Those are your charges."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Dismissive, closed tone
- ✗ Defensive posture
- ✗ No investigation offered
- ✗ No empathy or problem-solving

## FD-05: Handling complaint in second-language English

**SITUATION:**  
Guest with limited English explains air conditioning is broken. Frustrated and struggling with language.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Speaks slowly and clearly, avoids jargon, confirms understanding by repeating back, shows genuine empathy, apologises for discomfort, offers clear solution.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "I understand. Your air conditioning—the cold machine—it was not working. You were hot all night and could not sleep. I am very sorry. This is not good. Let me help you. We can do two things: I can send our engineer to your room right now to fix it, or we can move you to another room with good air conditioning. Which is better for you?"

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Simple, direct vocabulary
- ✓ Slow, measured pace
- ✓ Repetition back for confirmation
- ✓ Genuine empathy expression
- ✓ Multiple clear, simple options
- ✓ Appropriate to listener's English level

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Your AC is broken and you were uncomfortable. I'm sorry about that. We can fix it or move you to another room. What would you prefer?"

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Even simpler language
- ✗ More empathy expression
- ✗ Slower, more measured pace

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Yeah, we'll get maintenance to your room. Or you can switch to another one."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Too fast paced
- ✗ Jargon ("maintenance")
- ✗ No apology
- ✗ No empathy shown

## FD-06: Overbooking—walking guest to another hotel

**SITUATION:**  
Loyal repeat guest overbooked. No room available. Must walk to partner hotel at hotel's expense.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Takes ownership immediately, delivers bad news with empathy, presents alternative as quality option, offers concrete compensation, follows up with personal touch.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "I take full responsibility for this, and I sincerely apologize. You've been a loyal guest, and you deserve better than this. Here's what I'm going to do: I'm arranging for a taxi to take you to our sister property, the Grand Hotel, just five minutes away. They have a beautiful room ready for you right now. We're covering your full stay there tonight, plus I'm adding a $100 credit for your next stay with us here. And I'll personally call you tomorrow morning to make sure you're comfortable, and to discuss how we make this right going forward."

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Immediate ownership without blame-shifting
- ✓ Sincere, personal apology
- ✓ Quality alternative clearly presented
- ✓ Specific, generous compensation ($100)
- ✓ Personal follow-up commitment
- ✓ Discussion of longer-term resolution

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "I'm really sorry about this. We're walking you to the Grand Hotel, which is a good property nearby. We'll cover your stay and give you a discount on your next visit."

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Specific compensation amount
- ✗ Personal follow-up call
- ✗ Detailed alternative presentation

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "We don't have a room. You'll need to go to another hotel. We'll pay for your room."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ No ownership
- ✗ No apology
- ✗ Minimal compensation detail
- ✗ No empathy

## FD-07: Local recommendations / concierge ask

**SITUATION:**  
Couple asks for romantic restaurant recommendations. Want something local, not touristy, walking distance.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Gives 2-3 specific recommendations with personal details, asks follow-up questions, offers to help with reservations, shows genuine enthusiasm about the area.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Oh, I have some great ideas! First—there's a little place called Trattoria Lucia about a 10-minute walk from here. They have incredible fresh pasta, and it's tucked away on a quiet street—very romantic, very local. The owner is lovely. I'd ask for a table by the window if the weather's nice. Second option: if you want something a bit more upscale, the Rooftop Garden at the Rosewood has stunning city views and amazing modern cuisine. Either way, I'd love to make a reservation for you. What time were you thinking, and do you have any dietary preferences I should mention?"

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Specific restaurant names with context
- ✓ Walking distance/location details
- ✓ Personal touches ("ask for window")
- ✓ Mix of options (casual to upscale)
- ✓ Follow-up questions about preferences
- ✓ Service offer (make reservation)
- ✓ Genuine enthusiasm shown

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "There's a nice Italian place called Trattoria Lucia that's romantic, and the Rooftop Garden at the Rosewood has great views. Either one would be good for an anniversary. I can book one for you if you'd like."

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Walking distance/location context
- ✗ Personal touches or insider details
- ✗ Follow-up questions about preferences
- ✗ Authentic enthusiasm

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "There are some good restaurants nearby. Let me give you a list."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Generic, unhelpful
- ✗ No genuine enthusiasm
- ✗ No personal knowledge shown
- ✗ Minimal effort

## FD-08: Phone reservation enquiry

**SITUATION:**  
Caller inquires about mid-week availability next month. Three-night stay. Comparing multiple hotels.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Greets warmly, asks about trip purpose, presents options clearly with benefits (not just prices), highlights what makes hotel special, offers to hold or send booking link.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Thank you so much for calling! I'd love to help you find the perfect fit. Mid-week next month sounds great. Before I show you rates, can I ask—is this a business trip, a getaway, or a special occasion? That helps me recommend the right room. Our standard rooms are $140 a night, but we also have junior suites at $189 with extra space, and they come with complimentary breakfast—which guests absolutely love. Plus, we have a new wellness center with a great gym and spa. Would you like me to hold some rooms while you compare, or I can email you a booking link?"

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Warm, enthusiastic greeting
- ✓ Discovery questions about trip purpose
- ✓ Benefit-focused presentation (not price-only)
- ✓ Hotel differentiators (breakfast, wellness center)
- ✓ Service offer (hold or booking link)
- ✓ Guest empowerment (choice)

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Hi! Great, I can help with that. For mid-week, we have standard rooms at $140 and suites at $189. Do you need anything special for your stay?"

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Warmth in greeting
- ✗ Hotel differentiators
- ✗ Specific benefits mentioned
- ✗ Service offer (hold/link)

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Standard rooms are $140, suites are $189. When do you need them?"

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Robotic tone
- ✗ Price-only focus
- ✗ No engagement or discovery
- ✗ No benefits mentioned

## FD-09: Guest welcome & first impression at check-in

**SITUATION:**  
First-time guest arrives. Everything is in order. Goal: make excellent first impression.

**SUCCESS CRITERIA:**  
Welcomes by name, shows genuine enthusiasm, provides key practical info without overwhelming, offers help with luggage, leaves guest feeling excited about stay.

---

### MAX SCORE (85-100)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Taylor, welcome! We're so glad you're here—thank you for choosing us for your first stay with us. Let me help you get settled. Quick essentials: breakfast is 6:30 to 10am in the dining room just through there, Wi-Fi is 'Hotel-Guest' with no password, and our gym and pool are on the third floor. If you need anything at all—restaurant reservations, local tips, extra pillows—just call the front desk. Do you need help with your luggage, or shall I call someone to bring it up for you?"

**KEY ELEMENTS:**
- ✓ Personal greeting using name
- ✓ Genuine enthusiasm
- ✓ Key practical info without overload
- ✓ Clear, actionable details
- ✓ Offers for assistance
- ✓ Warmth and hospitality

---

### GOOD SCORE (70-84)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Welcome, Taylor! I'm glad you're here. Breakfast is from 6:30 to 10am, and here's your key. If you need anything, let me know."

**MISSING ELEMENTS:**
- ✗ Enthusiasm
- ✗ Luggage assistance offer
- ✗ Practical details (Wi-Fi, amenities)
- ✗ Warmth

---

### POOR SCORE (<70)

**RESPONSE:**
> "Here's your key. You're in room 412. The elevator is over there."

**ISSUES:**
- ✗ Mechanical, unwelcoming tone
- ✗ No welcome or greeting
- ✗ Minimal information
- ✗ No warmth or hospitality
